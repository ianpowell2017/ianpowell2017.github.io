## Mac keyboard shortcuts
By pressing a combination of keys, you can do things that normally need a mouse, trackpad, or other input device.
To use a keyboard shortcut, hold down one or more modifier keys while pressing the last key of the shortcut. 

For example, to use the shortcut **Command-C** (**copy**), hold down <kbd>Command</kbd> + <kbd>C</kbd>, then release both keys. Mac menus and keyboards often use symbols for certain keys, including the modifier keys:

Keys
- <kbd>Command</kbd> <kbd>⌘</kbd>
- <kbd>Shift</kbd> <kbd>⇧</kbd>
- <kbd>Option</kbd> <kbd>⌥</kbd>
- <kbd>Control</kbd> <kbd>⌃</kbd>
- <kbd>Caps Lock</kbd> <kbd>⇪</kbd>
- <kbd>Fn</kbd>

If you're using a keyboard made for Windows PCs, use the <kbd>Alt</kbd> key instead of Option, and the **Windows** logo key instead of **Command**. Some Mac keyboards and shortcuts use special keys in the top row, which include icons for volume, display brightness, and other functions. 

Press the icon key to perform that function, or combine it with the <kbd>Fn</kbd> key to use it as an <kbd>F1</kbd>, <kbd>F2</kbd>, <kbd>F3</kbd> or other standard function key. 

To learn more shortcuts, check the menus of the app you're using. Every app can have its own shortcuts, and shortcuts that work in one app may not work in another. 

## Cut, copy, paste, and other common shortcuts
<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>X</kbd>	Cut: Remove the selected item and copy it to the Clipboard

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>C</kbd>	Copy the selected item to the Clipboard. This also works for files in the Finder

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>V</kbd>	Paste the contents of the Clipboard into the current document or app. This also works for files in the Finder

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>Z</kbd>	Undo the previous command. You can then press <kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Z</kbd> to Redo, reversing the undo command. In some apps, you can undo and redo multiple commands

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>A</kbd>	Select All items

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>F</kbd>	Find: Open a Find window, or find items in a document

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>G</kbd>	Find Again: Find the next occurrence of the item previously found. To find the previous occurrence, press <kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>G</kbd>

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>H</kbd>	Hide the windows of the front app. To view the front app but hide all other apps, press <kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>H</kbd>

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>M</kbd>	Minimize the front window to the Dock. To minimize all windows of the front app, press C<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>M</kbd>

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>N</kbd>	New: Open an new document or window

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>O</kbd>	Open the selected item, or open a dialog to select a file to open

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>P</kbd>	Print the current document

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>S</kbd>	Save the current document

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>W</kbd>	Close the front window. To close all windows of the app, press <kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>W</kbd>

<kbd>Command</kbd> - <kbd>Q</kbd>	Quit the app

<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Esc</kbd>	Force Quit: Choose an app to force quit. Or press <kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>Esc</kbd> and hold for 3 seconds to force just the front app to quit

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Space bar</kbd>	Spotlight: Show or hide the Spotlight search field. To perform a Spotlight search from a Finder window, press <kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Space bar</kbd>. If you use multiple input sources to type in different languages, these shortcuts change input sources instead of showing Spotlight

<kbd>Space bar</kbd>	Quick Look: Use Quick Look to preview the selected item

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Tab</kbd>	Switch apps: Switch to the next most recently used app among your open apps

<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Tilde (~)</kbd> Switch windows: Switch to the next most recently used window of the front app

<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>3</kbd> Screenshot: Take a screenshot of the entire screen. Learn more screenshot shortcuts

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Comma (,)</kbd>	Preferences: Open preferences for the front app

## Sleep, log out, and shut down shortcuts

### Power button 
- Tap to turn on your Mac or wake your Mac from sleep
- Hold for 1.5 seconds while your Mac is awake to display a dialog asking if you want to restart, sleep, or shut down. If you don't want to wait 1.5 seconds, press <kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>Power</kbd> button or <kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>Media Eject</kbd> 
- Hold for 5 seconds to force your Mac to turn off.


<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Power</kbd> button to force your Mac to restart

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Power button</kbd> or <kbd>Media Eject</kbd> to put your displays to sleep

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Media Eject</kbd> Quit all apps, then restart your Mac. If any open documents have unsaved changes, you'll be asked whether you want to save them

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–(<kbd>Power button</kbd> or <kbd>Media Eject</kbd>) Quit all apps, then shut down your Mac. If any open documents have unsaved changes, you'll be asked whether you want to save them

<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Q</kbd> Log out of your OS X user account. You'll be asked to confirm

<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Q</kbd> Log out of your OS X user account immediately, without being asked to confirm

## Document shortcuts
<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>B</kbd> Boldface the selected text, or turn boldfacing on or off

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>I</kbd> Italicize the selected text, or turn italics on or off

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>U</kbd> Underline the selected text, or turn underlining on or off

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>T</kbd> Show or hide the Fonts window

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>D</kbd> Select the Desktop folder from within an Open dialog or Save dialog

<kbd>Control</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>D</kbd> Show or hide the definition of the selected word

<kbd>Shift</kbd>-<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Colon (:)</kbd> Display the Spelling and Grammar window

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Semicolon (;)</kbd> Find misspelled words in the document

<kbd>Option</kbd>-<kbd>Delete</kbd> Delete the word to the left of the insertion point

<kbd>Control</kbd>-<kbd>H</kbd> Delete the character to the left of the insertion point. Or use Delete

<kbd>Control</kbd>-<kbd>D</kbd> Delete the character to the right of the insertion point. Or use <kbd>Fn</kbd>-<kbd>Delete</kbd>

<kbd>Fn</kbd>-<kbd>Delete</kbd> Forward delete on keyboards that don't have a Forward Delete key. Or use <kbd>Control</kbd>-<kbd>D</kbd>

<kbd>Control</kbd>-<kbd>K</kbd> Delete the text between the insertion point and the end of the line or paragraph

<kbd>Command</kbd>-<kbd>Delete</kbd> Select Delete or Don't Save in a dialog that contains a Delete or Don't Save button

<kbd>Fn</kbd>–<kbd>Up Arrow</kbd> Page Up: Scroll up one page

<kbd>Fn</kbd>–<kbd>Down Arrow</kbd> Page Down: Scroll down one page

<kbd>Fn</kbd>–<kbd>Left Arrow</kbd> Home: Scroll to the beginning of a document

<kbd>Fn</kbd>–<kbd>Right Arrow</kbd> End: Scroll to the end of a document

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Up Arrow</kbd> Move the insertion point to the beginning of the document

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Down Arrow</kbd> Move the insertion point to the end of the document

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Left Arrow</kbd> Move the insertion point to the beginning of the current line

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Right Arrow</kbd> Move the insertion point to the end of the current line

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Left Arrow</kbd> Move the insertion point to the beginning of the previous word

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Right Arrow</kbd> Move the insertion point to the end of the next word

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Up Arrow</kbd> Select the text between the insertion point and the beginning of the document

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Down Arrow</kbd> Select the text between the insertion point and the end of the document

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Left Arrow</kbd> Select the text between the insertion point and the beginning of the current line

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Right Arrow</kbd> Select the text between the insertion point and the end of the current line

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Up Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection to the nearest character at the same horizontal location on the line above

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Down Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection to the nearest character at the same horizontal location on the line below

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Left Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection one character to the left

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Right Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection one character to the right

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Shift–Up Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection to the beginning of the current paragraph, then to the beginning of the following paragraph if pressed again

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Shift–Down Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection to the end of the current paragraph, then to the end of the following paragraph if pressed again

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Shift–Left Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection to the beginning of the current word, then to the beginning of the following word if pressed again

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Shift–Right Arrow</kbd> Extend text selection to the end of the current word, then to the end of the following word if pressed again

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>A</kbd> Move to the beginning of the line or paragraph

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>E</kbd> Move to the end of a line or paragraph

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>F</kbd> Move one character forward

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>B</kbd> Move one character backward

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>L</kbd> Center the cursor or selection in the visible area

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>P</kbd> Move up one line

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>N</kbd> Move down one line

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>O</kbd> Insert a new line after the insertion point

<kbd>Control</kbd>–<kbd>T</kbd> Swap the character behind the insertion point with the character in front of the insertion point

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Left Curly Bracket ({)</kbd> Left align

<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Right Curly Bracket (})</kbd> Right align

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command–Vertical bar (|)</kbd> Center align

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Command-F</kbd> Go to the search field

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Command-T</kbd> Show or hide a toolbar in the app

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Command-C</kbd> Copy Style: Copy the formatting settings of the selected item to the Clipboard

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Command-V</kbd> Paste Style: Apply the copied style to the selected item

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Shift-Command-V</kbd> Paste and Match Style: Apply the style of the surrounding content to the item pasted within that content

<kbd>Option</kbd>–<kbd>Command-I</kbd> Show or hide the inspector window

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>-P</kbd> Page setup: Display a window for selecting document settings

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>-S</kbd> Display the Save As dialog, or duplicate the current document

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>–Minus sign (-)</kbd> Decrease the size of the selected item

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>–Plus sign (+)</kbd> Increase the size of the selected item. <kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Equal sign (=)</kbd> performs the same function

<kbd>Shift</kbd>–<kbd>Command</kbd>–<kbd>Question mark (?)</kbd> Open the Help menu
